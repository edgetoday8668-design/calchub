How Much Mulch Do I Need?

Mulch is one of the most cost-effective ways to improve your garden — it retains moisture, suppresses weeds, and regulates soil temperature. Getting the right amount prevents waste and ensures proper coverage.

Recommended Mulch Depth

Bulk vs Bagged Mulch

For areas under 2 cubic yards, bagged mulch from a hardware store is convenient. For larger projects, bulk mulch delivered by the cubic yard is significantly cheaper — often 50% less per cubic yard than bagged. A standard pickup truck holds about 2 cubic yards of mulch.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bags of mulch do I need for a cubic yard?
One cubic yard equals 27 cubic feet. Standard bagged mulch comes in 2 cubic foot bags, so you need approximately 13–14 bags per cubic yard. Some bags are 3 cubic feet, requiring about 9 bags per cubic yard.
How deep should mulch be?
3 inches is the ideal depth for most garden beds — deep enough to suppress weeds and retain moisture, but not so deep it starves plant roots of oxygen. Never pile mulch against tree trunks or plant stems.
How often should I replace mulch?
Organic mulch like wood chips decomposes over 1–2 years and should be topped up annually. Add 1–2 inches each spring rather than replacing entirely, as decomposed mulch improves soil quality.
What is the best type of mulch for gardens?
Shredded hardwood bark is the most popular all-purpose mulch. Cedar mulch naturally repels insects. Pine bark is great for acid-loving plants. Rubber mulch lasts longest but doesn't improve soil. Straw is best for vegetable gardens.
