How to Calculate Concrete for Footings

Footings are the base of any structure — decks, fences, pergolas, and sheds all rely on properly sized concrete footings. Getting the volume right prevents wasted trips and ensures structural integrity.

Footing Size Guide by Project

ProjectDiameterDepth
Fence post8–10 inchesBelow frost line
Deck post (light)10–12 inchesBelow frost line
Deck post (heavy)12–16 inchesFrost line +6"
Pergola post12 inches24–36 inches
Shed foundation12 inchesBelow frost line

Always Check Your Local Frost Line

Frost line depth varies by location. In warm climates footings need 12 inches deep. In cold northern regions, frost lines can be 48–60 inches deep. Footings above the frost line will heave and crack in winter.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate concrete for footings?
For round footings: volume = π × (diameter/2)² × depth. For square: length × width × depth. Convert cubic inches to cubic feet by dividing by 1728, then divide by 27 for cubic yards.
How deep should concrete footings be?
Footings must extend below the frost line for your area. In warm climates 12 inches is minimum. In cold northern regions this can be 36–60 inches. Always check your local building code.
Can I mix concrete by hand for footings?
Yes — for small projects with fewer than 10 footings, hand-mixing bagged concrete is practical. For larger projects, renting a concrete mixer saves significant time and effort.
