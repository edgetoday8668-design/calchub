How to Calculate Concrete for Footings

Footings are the base of any structure — decks, fences, pergolas, sheds, and additions all rely on properly sized concrete footings. Getting the volume right prevents wasted trips to the hardware store and ensures your structure is properly supported.

Round vs Square Footings

Round tube footings (using cardboard Sonotube forms) are the most common for deck posts and fence posts. Square footings are typically used for heavier structural loads or where forms are built from lumber. Both types are supported by our calculator.

Always Check Your Local Frost Line

The frost line depth varies significantly by location. In warm climates like Florida or Texas, footings only need to go 12 inches deep. In colder northern states or Canada, frost lines can be 48–60 inches deep. Footings above the frost line will heave and crack in winter.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate concrete for footings?
For round footings: volume = π × (diameter/2)² × depth. For square footings: length × width × depth. Convert cubic inches to cubic feet by dividing by 1728, then to cubic yards by dividing by 27. Our calculator does all of this automatically.
How deep should concrete footings be?
Footings must extend below the frost line for your area to prevent frost heave. In warm climates, 12 inches is the minimum. In cold northern regions, this can be 36–60 inches. Always check your local building code before digging.
How many bags of concrete do I need per footing?
A standard 12-inch diameter footing at 36 inches deep needs about 0.098 cubic yards — roughly 4 bags of 60 lb concrete mix. Use our calculator above to get exact bag counts for any size.
Can I mix concrete by hand for footings?
Yes — for small projects with fewer than 10 footings, hand-mixing bagged concrete is practical. For larger projects, renting a concrete mixer saves significant time and effort. Ready-mix delivery is only cost-effective for very large footing projects.
