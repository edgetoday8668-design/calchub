How to Calculate Roofing Materials

Estimating roofing materials accurately prevents costly over-ordering or frustrating shortages mid-project. Our free roofing calculator accounts for roof pitch, number of sides, and a wastage buffer to give you a realistic material estimate.

What is a Roofing Square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet of roof surface. All roofing materials — shingles, felt underlayment, ice barrier — are sold and priced by the square. Most standard asphalt shingle bundles cover approximately 33 square feet, meaning you need 3 bundles per square.

Why Roof Pitch Matters

A steeper roof has more actual surface area than its footprint suggests. A flat 4/12 pitch roof covering a 40×20 ft footprint has significantly more surface area than a flat ceiling of the same size. Our calculator applies the correct pitch multiplier automatically.

Types of Roofing Shingles

Asphalt shingles are the most common and affordable option, lasting 20–30 years. Architectural (dimensional) shingles cost more but last longer and look better. Metal roofing lasts 40–70 years but has higher upfront cost. Always confirm shingle type with your supplier before ordering.

Don't Forget Underlayment

Roofing felt (underlayment) is installed beneath shingles to provide a moisture barrier. Standard 15 lb felt rolls cover approximately 400 square feet per roll. Heavier 30 lb felt provides better protection in high-wind or high-rainfall areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate how many shingles I need?
Divide your total roof area by 100 to get roofing squares. Each square needs 3 bundles of standard shingles. Add 10–15% for waste from cutting around edges, valleys, and ridges. Our calculator handles all of this automatically.
What is a roofing square?
A roofing square equals exactly 100 square feet of roof surface area. It is the standard unit used by roofers and material suppliers to measure and price roofing jobs. Three standard bundles of asphalt shingles cover one roofing square.
How much does a new roof cost?
Material costs for asphalt shingles average $1.50–$3.00 per square foot. Labour adds another $1.50–$3.00 per square foot. Total installed cost typically ranges $5–$8.50 per square foot depending on pitch, location, and roofing type.
How do I measure my roof pitch?
Roof pitch is the rise over 12 inches of run. Use a level and tape measure: place the level horizontally against the roof, measure 12 inches along it, then measure the vertical rise from that point to the roof surface. A 4-inch rise = 4/12 pitch.
How much wastage should I add for roofing?
Add 10% for simple gable roofs with few cuts. Add 15% for roofs with hips, valleys, or skylights. Add up to 20% for complex roofs with multiple angles and dormers. It is always cheaper to return unused materials than to make an emergency run mid-project.
