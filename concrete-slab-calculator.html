How to Calculate Concrete for a Slab

Whether you're pouring a driveway, patio, sidewalk, or garage floor, knowing exactly how much concrete you need saves money and prevents costly delays. The formula is Length × Width × Depth = Volume.

Standard Slab Thickness Guide

Project TypeThicknessNotes
Sidewalks3–4 inchesLight foot traffic
Patios & Driveways4 inchesStandard residential
Garage Floors4–6 inchesPassenger vehicles
Heavy Vehicles6 inchesAdd rebar reinforcement
Industrial Floors6–8 inchesRequires engineering review

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate how much concrete I need for a slab?
Multiply length × width × depth. In imperial, divide by 27 to get cubic yards. Our calculator does this instantly including a wastage buffer.
How many bags of concrete for a 10x10 slab?
A 10×10 ft slab at 4 inches thick = 1.23 cubic yards = approximately 46 bags of 60 lb mix. Always buy a few extra bags.
Can I download the calculation as a PDF?
Yes! After calculating, click "Download PDF" to get a professional report including your dimensions, results, and any remarks you add. Perfect for sharing with contractors or clients.
What is the Project Group feature?
