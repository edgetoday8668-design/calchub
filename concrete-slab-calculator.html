How to Calculate Concrete for a Slab

Whether you're pouring a driveway, patio, sidewalk, or garage floor, knowing exactly how much concrete you need saves you money and prevents costly delays. Our free concrete slab calculator makes this instant — no manual math required.

The Concrete Formula

The basic formula for concrete volume is: Length × Width × Depth = Volume. For imperial measurements, this gives you cubic feet. Divide by 27 to convert to cubic yards, since concrete is ordered by the cubic yard from ready-mix suppliers.

Bags vs. Ready-Mix Concrete

For small projects under 1 cubic yard, bagged concrete mix from a hardware store is more practical. For anything larger, ordering ready-mix concrete by the truck is cheaper and far less labour-intensive. A standard concrete truck holds 8–10 cubic yards.

Always Add a Wastage Buffer

Always order 5–10% more concrete than your calculation shows. Uneven subgrade, spillage, and slight measurement errors are common. Running short mid-pour can cause structural weak points in your slab. Our calculator includes an adjustable wastage buffer for this reason.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate how much concrete I need for a slab?
Multiply the length × width × depth (thickness) of the slab. In imperial, this gives cubic feet — divide by 27 to get cubic yards. Our calculator does all of this instantly, including a wastage buffer.
How many bags of concrete do I need for a 10x10 slab?
A 10×10 ft slab at 4 inches thick = 1.23 cubic yards. That's approximately 46 bags of 60 lb mix, or 37 bags of 80 lb mix. Always buy a few extra bags as buffer.
What thickness should a concrete slab be?
Standard residential patios and driveways are 4 inches. Sidewalks can be 3–4 inches. Garage floors and areas with heavy vehicle traffic should be 5–6 inches. Industrial use typically requires 6–8 inches with rebar.
How much does a cubic yard of concrete cost?
Ready-mix concrete typically costs $120–$200 per cubic yard in the US, depending on your location, mix strength, and supplier. Delivery fees and minimum order charges may also apply. Bagged concrete from a hardware store costs roughly $5–$7 per 60 lb bag.
Do I need rebar in my concrete slab?
For slabs that carry vehicles, heavy loads, or sit on unstable soil, rebar reinforcement is strongly recommended. Residential foot-traffic patios and sidewalks can often skip rebar, but wire mesh is still a good idea for crack prevention.
