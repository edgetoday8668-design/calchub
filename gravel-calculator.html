How to Calculate How Much Gravel You Need

Whether you're covering a driveway, pathway, garden bed, or drainage area, getting the right amount of gravel prevents costly over-ordering or frustrating top-up trips. The formula is: length × width × depth ÷ 27 = cubic yards.

Cubic Yards vs Tons — Which to Order?

Small suppliers often sell gravel by the bag (weight). Larger landscaping and stone suppliers sell by cubic yard or by the ton. When ordering by ton, the weight varies by material — crushed limestone is heavier than pea gravel at the same volume. Our calculator shows both so you can order in whichever unit your supplier uses.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many cubic yards of gravel do I need for a driveway?
For a standard 2-car driveway (20×20 ft) at 4 inches deep, you need approximately 5 cubic yards or about 7 tons of crushed stone. Add a 10% buffer for settling.
How much does a cubic yard of gravel weigh?
A cubic yard of gravel weighs approximately 2,800–3,400 lbs (1.4–1.7 tons) depending on material type and moisture. Crushed limestone is heavier; pea gravel and river rock are lighter.
How deep should gravel be for a driveway?
A gravel driveway needs at least 4 inches of depth. Ideally, use a 6-inch compacted base layer of crushed stone topped with 2–3 inches of decorative gravel. This provides drainage and stability.
What is the difference between gravel and crushed stone?
Natural gravel is rounded from water erosion, while crushed stone is mechanically broken and has angular edges. Crushed stone packs more tightly and is better for driveways. Natural gravel is better for drainage and decorative use.
