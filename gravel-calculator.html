How to Calculate How Much Gravel You Need

The formula is: length × width × depth ÷ 27 = cubic yards. Always add 10% for compaction and settling over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many cubic yards of gravel for a driveway?
For a standard 2-car driveway (20×20 ft) at 4 inches deep, you need approximately 5 cubic yards or about 7 tons of crushed stone. Add a 10% buffer for settling.
How much does a cubic yard of gravel weigh?
A cubic yard of gravel weighs approximately 2,800–3,400 lbs (1.4–1.7 tons) depending on material type and moisture. Crushed limestone is heavier; pea gravel is lighter.
How deep should gravel be for a driveway?
A gravel driveway needs at least 4 inches of depth. Ideally use a 6-inch compacted base topped with 2–3 inches of decorative gravel for drainage and stability.
