How to Calculate How Many Tiles You Need

Calculating tiles correctly saves money and prevents the frustration of running short mid-project. The basic formula is: divide the total room area by the area of one tile, then add a wastage percentage for cuts and breakage.

Wastage by Layout Pattern

Straight grid layouts waste the least material — typically 10%. Diagonal layouts require more cuts at edges, adding 15% waste. Complex patterns like herringbone can waste up to 20% due to angled cuts on every edge piece.

Don't Forget Grout and Adhesive

For every 50 square feet of tile, budget approximately one 25 lb bag of tile adhesive and one 10 lb bag of grout. Grout colour significantly affects the final look — always test on a small section first.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many tiles do I need for a 10x10 room?
A 10×10 ft room is 100 sq ft. Using 12×12 inch tiles (1 sq ft each), you need 100 tiles plus 10% wastage = 110 tiles. For 18×18 tiles, you need about 45 tiles plus wastage.
How much extra tile should I buy?
Add 10% for straight patterns, 15% for diagonal, and 20% for herringbone or complex layouts. Always buy a few extra boxes to keep in storage — matching tiles from the same batch becomes difficult later.
How do I calculate tile for a wall?
Measure height × width of the wall area in feet to get square footage. Subtract area of windows and doors. Divide by tile area and add 15% wastage for wall tiles since there are more edge cuts.
How many tiles come in a box?
It varies by tile size and manufacturer. Small mosaic tiles may have 20+ per box. Large format 24×24 tiles may have only 4 per box. Always check the box label for coverage area in square feet.
