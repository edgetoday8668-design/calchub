How to Calculate How Many Tiles You Need

The basic formula is: divide the total room area by the area of one tile, then add a wastage percentage for cuts and breakage.

Don't Forget Grout and Adhesive

For every 50 square feet of tile, budget approximately one 25 lb bag of tile adhesive and one 10 lb bag of grout.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many tiles do I need for a 10x10 room?
A 10×10 ft room is 100 sq ft. Using 12×12 inch tiles, you need 100 tiles plus 10% wastage = 110 tiles total.
How much extra tile should I buy?
Add 10% for straight patterns, 15% for diagonal, and 20% for herringbone. Always buy a few extra boxes to store for future repairs.
How do I calculate tile for a wall?
Measure height × width of the wall in feet. Subtract area of windows and doors. Divide by tile area and add 15% wastage for wall tiles.
