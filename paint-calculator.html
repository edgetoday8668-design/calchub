How to Calculate How Much Paint You Need

Running out of paint mid-project or buying too much both waste money. The key is calculating your wall area accurately, subtracting doors and windows, then multiplying by number of coats.

One Coat vs Two Coats

Two coats are almost always worth it — they provide better colour depth, durability, and even coverage. One coat may be enough when using paint-and-primer combinations on already-painted surfaces in similar colours. Going from light to dark or dark to light always needs two coats minimum.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much paint do I need for a 12x12 room?
A 12×12 room with 8 ft ceilings has approximately 320 sq ft of wall area (minus doors and windows, roughly 280 net sq ft). At 350 sq ft per gallon, you need about 1 gallon per coat — 2 gallons total for two coats.
How many square feet does a gallon of paint cover?
A gallon typically covers 350–400 sq ft on smooth surfaces with one coat. Textured, rough, or porous surfaces reduce this to 250–300 sq ft. Always check the specific product label for actual coverage rates.
Should I paint the ceiling the same colour as the walls?
Painting ceilings the same colour as walls creates a cocooning effect that makes rooms feel intimate and modern. Traditional white ceilings make rooms feel taller and brighter. Either way, use ceiling-specific paint for better coverage and a flat finish that hides imperfections.
What is the difference between paint finishes?
Flat/matte finish hides imperfections best — ideal for ceilings and low-traffic walls. Eggshell and satin are wipeable — good for living rooms and bedrooms. Semi-gloss and gloss are durable and moisture-resistant — ideal for trim, kitchens, and bathrooms.
