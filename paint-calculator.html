How to Calculate How Much Paint You Need

The key is calculating your wall area accurately, subtracting doors and windows, then multiplying by number of coats.

Paint Coverage Guide

Surface TypeCoverage per Gallon
Smooth drywall350–400 sq ft
Previously painted wall300–350 sq ft
Rough or textured wall250–300 sq ft
Bare wood or primer coat200–300 sq ft
Ceiling paint300–350 sq ft

One Coat vs Two Coats

Two coats provide better colour depth, durability, and even coverage. Going from light to dark or dark to light always needs two coats minimum.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much paint do I need for a 12x12 room?
A 12×12 room with 8 ft ceilings has approximately 280 net sq ft of wall area after subtracting doors and windows. At 350 sq ft per gallon, you need about 2 gallons for two coats.
How many square feet does a gallon of paint cover?
A gallon typically covers 350–400 sq ft on smooth surfaces with one coat. Textured or porous surfaces reduce this to 250–300 sq ft. Always check the product label.
What is the difference between paint finishes?
Flat/matte hides imperfections — ideal for ceilings. Eggshell and satin are wipeable — good for living rooms and bedrooms. Semi-gloss is moisture-resistant — ideal for trim, kitchens, and bathrooms.
